A cyber attack is any attempt by hackers or malicious actors to gain unauthorized access to your computer, smartphone, accounts, or data. These attacks can steal sensitive information, damage your system, or disrupt your digital life.

Step 1: Understand the Types of Cyber Attacks

Phishing: Fraudulent emails or messages that trick you into revealing passwords or clicking malicious links.
Malware: Software like viruses, ransomware, or spyware that harms your device or steals data.
Data Breach: When hackers gain access to private databases and expose your personal info.
Denial-of-Service (DoS): Overloading a website or service to make it unavailable.

Step 2: Protect Yourself Proactively

  • Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Combine letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid reusing passwords.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds an extra layer by requiring a code on your phone.
  • Keep Software Updated: Updates patch security holes hackers exploit.
  • Install Antivirus Software: Protects your device against malware and viruses.
  • Be Careful with Emails & Links: Don‚Äôt click suspicious links or download unknown attachments.
  • Secure Your Wi-Fi Network: Use strong passwords and WPA3 encryption if possible.

Step 3: What To Do If You Are Attacked

  • Disconnect from the Internet: Stop the attack from spreading or communicating.
  • Change Your Passwords Immediately: Use a secure device to do this.
  • Scan Your Device: Use antivirus and anti-malware software.
  • Notify Your Contacts: Warn friends or colleagues if your accounts were compromised.
  • Report the Incident: Contact your organization's IT team or local authorities.
  • Restore from Backups: If your data is encrypted or lost, recover from clean backups.
