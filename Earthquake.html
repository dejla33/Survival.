Earthquake Survival Guide

1. What Is an Earthquake?

Earthquakes are sudden ground movements caused by shifting tectonic plates, releasing seismic energy. They may cause structural damage, injuries, or fatalities.

2. Before an Earthquake

  • Secure heavy furniture to walls with brackets.
  • Identify safe spots like under sturdy tables or against interior walls.
  • Create and practice "Drop, Cover & Hold On" drills.
  • Prepare an emergency kit (water, food, meds, flashlight, documents).
  • Set family meeting points inside and outside.

3. During an Earthquake

  • Drop to the ground immediately.
  • Take cover under sturdy furniture.
  • Hold on tightly and protect your head.
  • Stay indoors and avoid windows and glass.
  • If outside, move to open spaces away from buildings.

4. After an Earthquake

  • Check for injuries and provide first aid.
  • Be alert for aftershocks.
  • Turn off utilities if damaged (gas, electricity).
  • Avoid damaged buildings and stay safe outdoors.
  • Use radio or battery-powered devices for updates.
  • Help neighbors if it is safe.

5. Emergency Kit Checklist

  • Water: 1 gallon per person per day.
  • Non-perishable food for at least 3 days.
  • Flashlight and spare batteries.
  • First-aid supplies and medicines.
  • Whistle to signal for help.
  • Portable phone charger and extra batteries.
  • Important documents in waterproof bag.
