First Aid Training

Learn essential first aid skills to help save lives in emergencies. Follow the detailed instructions below and watch the videos for better understanding.

Bleeding Control

Controlling bleeding quickly can prevent shock and save a life. Hereâ€™s what to do:

  • Apply firm, direct pressure on the wound using a clean cloth or bandage.
  • If possible, elevate the injured limb above heart level to reduce bleeding.
  • Maintain pressure until bleeding stops or emergency help arrives.
  • Use a tourniquet only if direct pressure is insufficient and you are trained to use it.

Burns

Treat burns carefully to avoid infection and reduce damage:

  • Remove the person from the source of the burn immediately.
  • Cool the burn area with running cool (not cold) water for 10-20 minutes.
  • Cover the burn with a sterile, non-stick dressing or clean cloth.
  • Do NOT apply creams, oils, or ice directly to the burn.
  • Seek medical help for large, deep, or severe burns.

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

CPR can save lives by maintaining blood flow to the brain and heart when the heart stops:

  • Check responsiveness and breathing of the person.
  • If unresponsive and not breathing normally, call emergency services immediately.
  • Start chest compressions: place hands on the center of the chest and press hard and fast (about 100-120 compressions per minute).
  • Give rescue breaths if trained; otherwise, continue compressions until help arrives.
  • Use an AED (Automated External Defibrillator) if available and trained.
